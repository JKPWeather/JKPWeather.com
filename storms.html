Tropics as of 7/25

As Hurricane Hanna lumbers inland and weakens to a tropical depression, all eyes turn to Invest 92L. It formed from an anomalously strong and large AEW (African Easterly Wave), and moved into the Main Development Region on Wednesday, July 22. While the general environment in the MDR is still hostile, leading to the demise of Gonzalo, a sacrificial wave ahead of 92L has moistened the environment for now. The models are showing a strong signal of development, and the NHC has given the storm a 90% chance of development over the next 5 days. A wave axis, an elongated low pressure system, has formed in 92L, and if it can tighten up it will strengthen. While that is very likely to happen, the faster that happens, the higher potential impacts. If 92L (soon to be Isias) can strengthen quickly, it will stay north of the Caribbean and move towards the East Coast of the US. If it takes longer to strengthen, it will interact with land in the Greater Antilles, which would prevent significant strengthening. There is a lot of time to watch this system, but all interests on the East Coast should be aware.
